---
sutra: हलो यमां यमि लोपः
vRtti: हल उत्तरेषां यमां यमि परतो लोपो भवति अन्यतरस्याम् ॥
vRtti_eng: After a consonant, the following semi-vowel or a nasal is elided optionally, when the same letters follow it.

---
The word अन्यतरस्याम् should be read into the _sutra_. Thus शय्य्या or शय्या, the middle य् being elided. In शय्या there are two यs, one of the affix क्यप् (III.3.99); and the second of अयङ् (VII.4.22), and the third arises by doubling (VIII.4.47). आदित्य्य (formed by doubling य by the _Vartika_ under 47 _ante_), or आदित्य 'son of _Aditi_' In आदित्य्य there are two यs, one of ण्य (IV.1.85), and the second arises by doubling.

Similarly आदित्य देवता अस्य स्थाली पाकस्य = आदित्य्य ॥ Then by (VIII.4.47), there is a third य, as अदित्य्य ॥ Here also we may elide the one middle य or both the middle य's. Thus आदित्यः or आदित्य्य ॥

Why do we say हलः 'preceded by a consonant'? Observe अन्नम् when म् is preceded by a vowel.

Why do we say यमाम् "of यम् letters"? Observe अग्ग्निः, अर्घ्यम् (V.4.25) where घ is not a य ॥

Why do we say यमिं "when यम् follows" ? Observe शार्ङ्गम् here ङ् is not followed by a यम् ॥
